Ubuntu Bali · Philosophy

The Strength of the Circle

Why I Am Requires We Are

We live in a culture obsessed with the Self. We celebrate the self-made billionaire, the solo adventurer, and the individual spark of genius. We have been conditioned to believe that our success is a ladder we climb alone. But there is a profound African philosophy that suggests we have been looking at the world through a cracked lens.

"

I am because we are.

Ubuntu

The Illusion of the Individual

In the West, we often follow the Cartesian logic of "I think, therefore I am." It places the center of the universe squarely between our own ears. But if you take a human being and strip away every relationship, every teacher, every parent, every colleague — what is left?

The truth is that none of us exists in a vacuum. A leader is not a leader without a team that chooses to follow. Ubuntu reminds us that we do not find ourselves by looking inward. We find ourselves by looking at the people around us.

The Ecosystem of Humanity

Imagine a forest. We might point to a single, towering oak and marvel at its strength. But beneath the soil, that tree is part of a complex, invisible network. If the forest dies, the tree dies — no matter how strong it thinks it is.

Your success is my success.
If the team wins, the individual thrives.
Your suffering is my suffering.
If a member is marginalized, the integrity of the whole is compromised.
Kindness is a survival strategy.
Treating others with dignity is the only way to sustain the environment that allows us to exist.

Leading with Ubuntu

When a leader says "I am because we are," they acknowledge that their authority is a gift from the people they serve. Their job is not to be the smartest person in the room, but to create a room where everyone feels safe enough to be their best selves.

"I am because we are."

It is not just a philosophy.
It is a biological and social reality.
